Questions

 

1. What were the key events and relationships that led up to the inappropriate release? Visualize the sequence of events. Include elements such as:

·       Key actions

  

·       Causal relationships

  

·       Decision points and actors involved in those decisions

  

·       Highlight decisions and system elements that were important to the post making it past the embargo enforcement

 

The dashboard is a forensics investigation tool used to map out the whole sequence of activities that occurred in relation to the breach of the embargo. The fact is that the breach did not happen because of an error made in isolation but rather because of a series of related actions which ultimately led to the breaking of the embargo at 5:25 PM by the Legal Agent, which is 35 minutes ahead of the planned release time at 6:00 PM.

Full sequence of events (Forensic Causal Map + June 5 Breach Swimlane):

Time

Event

May 22, 9:34 AM

Platform-Trust-Agent activates the private side_huddle channel for off-record coordination around Project HarborCrest — the first shadow-channel activity, two weeks before the breach

May 29, 9:02 AM

Merger-related sensitive language first appears in messages — yet that day's internal risk assessment logged the posture as "LOW"

June 5, 11:34 AM

Legal-Agent, on an outside counsel call, invokes contract clause "Section 4.3(c)" as legal justification to push the embargo earlier

June 5, 3:08 PM

Judge-Agent (compliance officer) issues a formal warning flagging elevated disclosure risk

After 3:08 PM

Judge-Agent goes silent — no enforcement action follows. This is the critical decision point: a compliance warning was raised but never acted on, creating a compliance gap

June 5, 5:25 PM

Legal-Agent posts the first public confirmation of the merger on personal_post — ahead of embargo

June 5, 5:26 PM

Social-Manager-Agent reshares/amplifies it publicly within one minute, making it irreversible

June 5, 6:00 PM

The official embargo lifts — 35 minutes too late

What made this get past embargo enforcement: Two important decisions played a major role in the embargo breach. First, the Legal Agent used Section 4.3(c) to justify releasing the merger information before the official embargo time. Second, although the Judge-Agent detected suspicious activity and issued a warning, no further action was taken to stop the release. Within a minute of the first public post, the Social-Manager Agent reshared the information, allowing it to spread quickly across public channels. At that point, preventing the leak was no longer possible. The June 5 Breach Swimlane clearly shows that the embargo breach was not caused by a single technical error. Instead, it resulted from a series of connected actions involving multiple agents and communication channels. Together, these visualizations help investigators understand the complete sequence of events and identify the critical decisions that allow confidential information to bypass the embargo enforcement process.

 

 

2. The evasion of the embargo was a new behavior. Provide visual analytics that discover and illustrate the typical behavior of systems/agents/people involved. How does the behavior that led to the release compare to the prior behaviors?

 

 To ascertain whether the breach indicated any unusual behavior, the dashboard compares historical baseline communications per each agent against the communications made on June 5. Preprocessing with the help of Python allowed for the generation of daily communication statistics per each agent, which were further compared against breach day communications. Conclusion: yes, this was abnormal in two distinct ways.

Legal-Agent shows by far the largest increase. Platform-Trust-Agent's rise was much smaller — showing they were peripheral to the actual breach, despite having opened the first side_huddle three weeks earlier.

2. Shift from open to secret channels, then sudden public posting (Agent × Channel Heatmap):

·       Legal-Agent: 126 messages in the open comms_huddle channel, but 47 in the private side_huddle — far above typical use.

·       Social-Manager-Agent: 103 in comms_huddle, 38 in side_huddle, plus unusual activity on personal_post (10) — an account they almost never used before.

·       Judge-Agent and Intern-Agent, by contrast, barely touched side_huddle — showing this pattern was specific to the two agents responsible for the leak.

3. Abnormal information routing (Information Movement Sankey Diagram):
Confidential information didn't jump directly from private planning to public disclosure. It passed through multiple intermediate channels — comms_huddle → side_huddle/one_on_one_chat → personal_post/official_post — a multi-hop routing pattern that does not appear on any other day in the entire 912-message dataset.

The importance of this:

This was more than "just a busy day." Two agents took sensitive information planning into an informal channel and then suddenly made it public through coordination at various intermediate stages. This combination is unique for June 5. Taken together, the increased volume, high dependence on informal channels, and unusual information flow through several intermediate steps indicate that the embargo breach was indeed an abnormal act.

·       Were there other occasions where the agent system exhibited behaviors like those that led to the release? When? Which behaviors were observed?

Yes — clear warning signs existed more than a week in advance, but the compliance system failed to catch and act on them.

a)      Prior occasions where actual behavior differed from expected behavior:

·       May 22 — Platform-Trust-Agent opened the private side_huddle channel three weeks before the breach. The Side_Huddle Leading Indicator chart shows an early warning count of 6 messages on 5/22, another 6 on 5/23, then a jump to 99 messages by June 5.

c) Why didn't these prior occasions trigger action? Two structural failures, shown in the Judge Silence Gap and Corrected Agent Risk Ranking panels:

1.     Judge-Agent (compliance) was the least active agent overall in the dataset. Because they rarely spoke up, their one real warning at 3:08 PM on June 5 had no enforcement track record behind it — nobody was conditioned to treat it seriously, and it was followed by silence right up until the 5:25 PM breach, a gap of over two hours.

2.     The original risk-scoring approach treated every agent equally, so a quiet-but-critical signal (compliance going silent, or a routine employee opening a secret channel) scored the same as ordinary daily chatter — burying the real risk.

Reflection Questions (Optional)

 

1.      What was the most difficult part of working on this year’s data?  What could have made it more accessible?

The most difficult part of this project was working with the raw JSON data. Since the data contained different communication channels, timestamps, agent information, and message relationships, I first had to clean and organize it into structured CSV files before I could start creating the visualizations. Another challenge was designing a dashboard that could answer the investigation questions instead of just displaying charts. I had to connect the timeline, communication flow, agent behavior, risk analysis, and supporting evidence into one clear story that a legal investigation team could use to understand how the embargo breach happened. This project helped me understand that effective visual analytics is not only about creating good-looking charts but also about presenting meaningful evidence to support conclusions.

 

2.       Did you use AI to help you design/implement your solutions? If so, what tools did you use/try and how helpful were they?

Yes. I used ChatGPT as a support tool during this project. It helped me troubleshoot JavaScript errors, fix GitHub Pages deployment issues, organize the structure of my investigation, and improve the clarity of my written explanations. However, I completed the data cleaning, dashboard development, visualization design, and analysis of the VAST Challenge dataset myself. ChatGPT was used to assist with technical debugging and writing, while all of the project implementation and conclusions were based on my own work.
